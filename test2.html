Interstellar Astronomy Challenge

The Premier Global Astronomy Competition for 2024

About the Competition

The Interstellar Astronomy Challenge 2024 is the most prestigious astronomy competition for students and amateur astronomers worldwide. Organized by the International Astronomical Union, this event brings together the brightest minds to explore, discover, and compete in various cosmic challenges.

Participants will demonstrate their expertise through theoretical examinations, practical observation tests, and cutting-edge astrophysics problem-solving. The competition culminates in a grand finale at the European Space Astronomy Centre.

Competition Rules

  • Eligibility: Open to participants aged 14-25 worldwide. Both individual and team entries (max 3 members) are accepted.
  • Structure:
    • Qualifying Round: Online theoretical examination (90 minutes)
    • Regional Finals: Practical tests at designated observatories
    • Global Championship: Week-long competition featuring observation, data analysis, and research challenges
  • Evaluation: Judged by a panel of professional astronomers based on accuracy, methodology, and innovation.
  • Code of Conduct: Strict academic integrity policies enforced. All work must be original.
  • Equipment: Basic observational tools provided. Personal equipment may be used within specified guidelines.

Competition Topics

The 2024 challenge will focus on these key astronomical domains:

  • Planetary Science: Exoplanet detection methods, planetary atmospheres, and comparative planetology
  • Stellar Astrophysics: Stellar evolution models, nucleosynthesis, and variable star analysis
  • Galactic Dynamics: Dark matter distribution, galactic rotation curves, and structure formation
  • Observational Techniques: Photometry, spectroscopy, and radio astronomy data interpretation
  • Cosmology: Cosmic microwave background analysis, large-scale structure, and inflation theory
  • Space Mission Design: Orbital mechanics, spacecraft instrumentation, and mission planning
