Stellar Olympiad

A nationwide astronomy competition for students and enthusiasts to explore the wonders of the cosmos.

About the Competition

The Stellar Olympiad is an annual competition designed to test knowledge and passion for astronomy. Our mission is to inspire the next generation of astronomers, astrophysicists, and space explorers. Participants will engage in challenging theoretical questions, practical observation tasks, and a collaborative project component. This competition is open to high school students and university undergraduates who have a keen interest in the universe.

Rules and Regulations

  • Registration is mandatory and must be completed by the deadline.
  • The competition will consist of three rounds: a preliminary online quiz, a practical observation round, and a final presentation.
  • All participants must adhere to a code of conduct promoting fair play and respect.
  • Plagiarism and any form of cheating will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Individual and team participation options are available.
  • Winners will be selected based on a cumulative score across all rounds.

Competition Topics

🌌

Planetary Science

Formation, characteristics, and moons of planets in our solar system.

🌈

Stellar Astrophysics

Life cycles of stars, stellar evolution, and stellar spectra.

🌌

Cosmology

Big Bang theory, dark matter, dark energy, and the fate of the universe.

🌐

Observational Astronomy

Using telescopes, astrophotography, and celestial navigation.

🌟

Galaxies and Nebulae

Structure and classification of galaxies, nebulae, and black holes.

☄️

Exoplanets

Detection methods, habitability, and characteristics of planets outside our solar system.
